    Servysan

    The Project

    Servysan is a freelance contract for a sanitation company based in Puebla, Mexico. Servysan prides itself in its commitment to their environment and the quality of their services.

    The client needed a site for their company that emphasize what they offer while being relevant to their commitment to the environment. They had settled on a leaf and bright green to be the design motifs for their company but felt their image as a company lacking.

    Research & Development

    To start, I had to scrap the original logo provided as it was a non-vector image that was already too blurry. I collaborated with Nicole R.T from Old St Nick to help me redesign their logo into something more web friendly. Next was color. I opted to tone down the bright green they gave me and chose more earth tones to keep in line in what they want to represent.

    • Accent Color: #8CA031
    • Banner Color: #141414
    • Background Color: #ffffff
    • Font Color: ##000000

    Okay Munchy

    Okay Munchy is a website for my Database Theory class. The main focus of it is to display map and other relevant information over restaurants, coffee shops, and bars near our campus.

    We split the website in two parts. A database back end that handles the data and a front end that allows users to easily see the data being displayed. My contribution for the projected involved working on the front end of the website.

    My main focus when designing the front end was ease of use. I implemented various techniques to bring the site to life like: large font for easier reading. A good color palette to liven up the site and large icons to help users know what they are clicking.

    DM World Edit

    Dungeon Masters World Edit (DMWE)was a project born out the love (and frusturation) of playing Dungeons & Dragons with family and friends. Generating NPC is a tedious process only made more difficult when a DM needs to keep track of them during the course of a game. What started as a small app for creating simple NPCs quickly balloned into something larger.

    In its current iteration DMWE can now make simple NPCs and Items in addition with the ability to roll loot dice for generic items.

    The future of DMWE will include a clean UI instead of command line code with more complex code to handle random generation of NPCs and Items as opposed to manual creation.
